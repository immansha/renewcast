CENTRAL ELECTRICITY REGULATORY COMMISSION
MERIT ORDER DISPATCH RULES 2025
Effective: January 1, 2025

SECTION 1 - DISPATCH PRIORITY CLASSES

Class 1 - Must-Run Sources:
  Nuclear, run-of-river hydro, renewable energy (solar, wind).
  Dispatched first regardless of cost.

Class 2 - Hydro and Pumped Storage:
  Reservoir hydro, pumped storage. Fast ramp under 8 minutes.

Class 3 - Gas-Based Generation:
  CCGT and Open Cycle Gas. Ramp time 15-20 min CCGT, 5-10 min OCGT.
  Merit order within class by heat rate (lowest first).

Class 4 - Coal-Based Thermal:
  Dispatched last. Cold start: 4 hours notice. Warm start: 30 minutes.

SECTION 2 - RESERVE REQUIREMENTS

Spinning Reserve: Minimum 5% of total demand at all times.
For grids with renewable penetration above 40%:
  Spinning reserve increases to 10% of demand.

Frequency Regulation: plus or minus 0.2 Hz from 50 Hz nominal.
AGC response required within 30 seconds.

SECTION 3 - DEVIATION SETTLEMENT MECHANISM (DSM)

Deviation band: plus or minus 15% of scheduled output without penalty.
Beyond 15%: INR 0.50 per kWh per percentage point of deviation.

SECTION 4 - RENEWABLE ENERGY INTEGRATION

4.1 Solar Integration Protocol:
Maintain backup equal to 30% of scheduled solar during peak solar hours
(10:00 to 15:00 IST).

4.2 Ramp Event Protocol:
When solar forecast drops more than 20% within 30 minutes:
  - Immediately notify backup assets
  - Pre-position gas backup within ramp range
  - Maintain 10% spinning reserve above normal requirement

4.3 Forecasting Requirements:
Solar generators above 10 MW must submit:
  - Day-ahead forecast by 10:00 IST previous day
  - Intra-day update every 30 minutes during generation hours

SECTION 5 - PENALTIES

Failure to comply with merit order dispatch without valid technical reason:
  First offense: Warning
  Subsequent offenses: INR 5,00,000 per incident
